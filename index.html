Mapping Mahaweli

Writing the World from a River

The Accelerated Mahaweli Development Program

It is more imperative than ever to understand the profound significance of water as one of the most essential elements of life. Water plays a vital role in the global ecosystem, regulating the climate. Beyond being a life-sustaining compound (H2O), water carries complexities that extend beyond its basic chemical composition. Numerous cultural practices, values, and beliefs are associated with water across space and time as it shapes social and cultural experiences. Despite water being essential for life, it is not always (made) accessible or distributed equally among everyone. Because of the very value of water as a source of life and its socio-political and economic value, water can also be a source of conflict, as individuals, communities, and nation-states compete to access and control water resources. Due to the growing concerns of anthropogenic climate change, water has become a central topic in scholarly discussions. Given the centrality and inevitability of water in any given human society, cultural anthropologists have been interested in studying water.
