Writing the World from a River

The Accelerated Mahaweli Development Program

The Mahaweli River is the longest river in Sri Lanka that runs 208 miles, starting from the central highlands and flows across the country until it reaches the Bay of Bengal on the southwestern side of Trincomalee Bay. It carries the largest volume of water amounting to one seventh of Sri Lanka's total runoff and has a basin that covers 15 percent of the country's land.

The Mahaweli Development program is the largest multipurpose national development program in the history of Sri Lanka. Read more about the Development project here.

About Mapping Mahaweli

Mapping Mahaweli features a digital map that archives the historical milestones of the river Mahaweli and the Accelerated Mahaweli Development Project since 1961. This will provide a platform for the users to conveniently understand technopolitics of a river based development project which is often framed as a machine to bring in prosperity.

Mahaweli Memories are stories written based on my own ethnographic research in Dehiaththakandiya, Sri Lanka and based on archival data (e.g., newspaper articles). These stories will bring into focus various outcomes, social issues that emerge over the time as a result of this development project and stories people tell about their experiences around this development project.
