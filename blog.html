Sitting on a broken damro plastic chair, 68 years old started telling me about his illness. “They are telling us it is caused by the water we drink. But no one tells us what’s wrong with the water and what caused water go bad,” Gurunnnase, a 68 year-old man, who used to farm acres of rice fields and lead the village’s hewisi team told me when I asked what he thinks causes this kideny disease. His name is Rala, but everyone in the village called him Gurunnanse. He used to play all the musical instruments back in the days at temple, funerals, religious functions, and has trained many children. In other words, he was the teacher of many generations of hewisi groups in Meegahayaya. “I have many golayos (students). Now I have stopped everything, I don’t have the energy”, he says and walks inside the house and brings a plastic chair for me. “Oh, you don’t have to do that, I can sit here, by the steps.” He gives ne the chair anyways.
